Yes, it is possible to create a live streaming app that connects with any brand of 360 cameras and recording glasses simultaneously. However, there are several key considerations you'll need to address in the development process:

1. Hardware Compatibility

• 360 Cameras: Many 360 cameras have their own proprietary software or apps. To support multiple brands, your app will need to either: 

• Integrate with the APIs provided by various camera manufacturers.

• Support universal streaming protocols (like RTMP, RTSP, or HLS) that work with the cameras.

• Recording Glasses: Similarly, recording glasses might not all use the same protocol or app. Some may use Bluetooth or other wireless technologies, while others may rely on proprietary solutions. 

• Again, you'd need to integrate APIs or ensure compatibility with common standards like Bluetooth, RTMP streaming, or any proprietary protocols.

2. Streaming Technology

• Real-Time Streaming Protocols: To connect both types of devices simultaneously, you’ll need to integrate standard streaming protocols (RTMP, HLS, or WebRTC) to deliver the video from both devices to your live stream.

• Video Processing: Your app will need the ability to sync the video streams from multiple sources (360 camera and recording glasses) and ensure the video feeds are synchronized. This could be a challenge in terms of latency.

3. App Development

• Cross-Platform: Ensure your app can run on multiple platforms (iOS, Android, Windows) as different cameras or glasses may support different platforms.

• User Interface (UI): Design a UI that makes it easy for users to connect their devices (cameras and glasses) and control settings such as streaming quality, recording options, etc.

4. Cloud Storage & Bandwidth

• Cloud Integration: For storing and processing the live feeds (especially if users want to save their streams), your app will need integration with cloud services like AWS, Google Cloud, or custom servers.

• Bandwidth Requirements: Live streaming video, especially from multiple sources (360 camera and glasses), requires a lot of bandwidth. Your app must handle varying network conditions effectively.

5. Integration with Camera & Glasses APIs

• Each brand of camera and glasses may provide SDKs or APIs for integration, which allows you to stream the video to your app. Some popular brands for 360 cameras (e.g., GoPro, Insta360) and smart glasses (e.g., Google Glass, Spectacles by Snap) provide such SDKs.

• You'll need to work with these APIs or find a third-party solution to create the connection between the devices and your app.

6. Security & Privacy

• Data Encryption: Secure the video feeds, especially if they're being streamed to public platforms. Encrypt the data both during transmission and storage.

• User Permissions: If you’re connecting to wearable glasses, ensure that privacy concerns are considered, with clear permissions for recording and streaming.

7. User Experience (UX)

• The app should make connecting multiple devices seamless, offering real-time feedback on video quality, battery status, and connectivity.

• Users should be able to easily manage multiple live streams from different devices (e.g., 360-degree footage from the camera and POV footage from the glasses).

Steps to Build the App:

• Research Device Compatibility: Identify the brands and models you want to support and check if they provide APIs or SDKs for integration.

• Choose a Streaming Platform: Select the streaming protocols that work for all your devices (RTMP, WebRTC).

• Develop the App: Build the app using a cross-platform tool (e.g., Flutter, React Native) or native development for better hardware access.

• Test on Multiple Devices: Since you are dealing with various camera and glasses brands, extensive testing will be crucial to ensure everything works seamlessly.

• Launch and Monitor: After launching, continue to monitor and update the app for compatibility with new devices or firmware updates.

If you’re serious about this project, it might be beneficial to work with experienced developers, especially those with expertise in hardware integration and streaming technologies.

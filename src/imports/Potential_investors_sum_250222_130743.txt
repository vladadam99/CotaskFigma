The potential sum you could secure from investors depends on several factors, including the stage of development, the specific value proposition, the market opportunity, and the growth potential of your business. Here’s a breakdown to help estimate the possible investment range:

1. Seed Stage Investment (Pre-Revenue / Early Development)

• Investment Amount: Seed funding typically ranges from $100,000 to $2 million. At this stage, investors are mainly looking at your concept, the team, and the potential of the technology.

• Key Factors: Investors will assess the potential of your product (VR integration, 360° camera use, immersive experiences), the scalability of your business model, and the team’s ability to execute the plan.

• Equity Offered: Typically, at this stage, investors expect to take 10-20% equity in the company, depending on how much capital you need and your valuation.

2. Series A (Post-Prototype, Pre-Revenue or Early Revenue)

• Investment Amount: Series A funding typically ranges from $2 million to $10 million. This is where investors start to see tangible traction—either through early users, prototypes, or minimal revenue.

• Key Factors: Investors will focus on user growth, the technological feasibility of the VR integration, and the potential scalability of the business (both in terms of revenue and market penetration).

• Equity Offered: At this stage, you may offer around 15-30% equity in exchange for funding, depending on the valuation and amount you seek.

3. Series B and Beyond (Established Revenue, Expansion Phase)

• Investment Amount: Series B investments typically range from $10 million to $50 million or more. If you are in this stage, you likely have a proven business model, with consistent revenue and strong growth potential.

• Key Factors: Investors will look for solid revenue growth, market penetration, and proof of scalability. By this stage, you'd need to demonstrate that your immersive VR platform has significant traction in key markets like education, corporate training, and virtual services.

• Equity Offered: In exchange for this level of funding, equity offers could range from 10-20%, depending on your valuation and the strategic importance of the investment.

4. Valuation and Market Opportunity Considerations

• Market Opportunity: VR and immersive technology are high-growth markets. The global VR market is expected to reach $40 billion by 2026. Given this market potential, your product could easily position itself in a multi-million-dollar opportunity.

• Revenue Model: With multiple revenue streams, like subscriptions, licensing, and pay-per-task, you’re setting up a business model with recurring revenue potential. This could justify a higher valuation as investors would see long-term revenue generation.

Starting a business from scratch, especially one that involves technology like an immersive VR app, can feel overwhelming at first, but breaking it down into manageable steps can make it more manageable. Here’s a simple guide to help you get started:

1. Define Your Vision & Plan

Before diving into the technical side or seeking investment, it’s important to have a clear vision of what your business aims to achieve and how it will operate. You've already developed a solid business idea, but now focus on refining these key points:

• What is your value proposition? (Why will people use your app?)

• What problem does your app solve? (In your case, providing immersive task experiences through VR/360° camera integration.)

• What are your short-term and long-term goals?

You already have a business plan, which is a great start! Refine it, and keep it updated as you move forward.

2. Understand Your Market

To succeed, you need to understand the landscape you’re entering. This includes:

• Researching competitors: Look at other VR apps or services in the market. What makes your app different?

• Understanding your customers: Who are they, what are their needs, and why would they use your app?

• Validating your idea: You can do this by talking to potential users and gathering feedback. Try to get a sense of how much demand there is for your app.

3. Legal Protection (IP Protection)

As you have a unique business idea, protecting your intellectual property (IP) is crucial.

• Trademark: Register your brand name and logo to prevent others from using them.

• Copyright: Your code, designs, and content are automatically copyrighted once created. But keeping records and timestamps is helpful.

• Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA): If you need to share your idea with potential investors or partners, have them sign an NDA to protect your idea.

4. Build Your Product (MVP)

Start small with a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) — the simplest version of your app that demonstrates the core features. This will help you test the market and get feedback without investing too much upfront.

• Find a development team: You can either hire freelance developers or work with a development agency. Look for people who specialize in VR/360° technologies.

• Focus on core features: Build the essential features first, such as VR integration and camera streaming.

5. Fund Your Business

You’ll likely need funding to get started, especially for the development and hardware (VR headsets, cameras, etc.).

• Self-funding (Bootstrapping): If you have personal savings, you can use them to cover initial costs.

• Seek Investors: You can approach angel investors, venture capitalists, or even crowdfunding platforms. Create an attractive pitch deck (a visual summary of your business, financials, and market potential) to present to potential investors.

• Grants and competitions: Some organizations offer funding for tech startups. Research UK government grants or startup competitions you can enter.

6. Develop Your Marketing Plan

Marketing will be key to getting users and investors on board. Here’s how you can start:

• Create a website: Build a professional-looking website to showcase your idea and attract investors.

• Social Media & Content: Create social media accounts and start posting about VR, immersive technology, and your app. Share updates, progress, and upcoming launches.

• Demo videos: Create demo videos that highlight how immersive and unique your app is. Show both FPS and TPS modes with VR glasses for maximum impact.

7. Launch & Iterate

Once you have your MVP, launch it to a small group of users to gather feedback. This could be through:

• Beta Testing: Invite people to test your app and provide feedback.

• User Feedback: Use this feedback to make improvements before scaling up.

Once you have a more polished product, you can expand your marketing efforts and start building a larger user base.

Summary of Next Steps:

• Refine your business plan and define a clear vision.

• Protect your idea with IP (trademarks, copyright, NDA).

• Develop an MVP: Build the app’s core features and test it.

• Secure funding: Either through personal savings, investors, or grants.

• Market your app through a website, social media, and demo videos.

• Launch & gather feedback: Improve based on initial user responses.
